# v3 demo narration

Each numbered paragraph maps to the identically numbered browser clip produced by
`scripts/capture_demo.py`. This cut is intentionally product-first and targets 78–92 seconds.

## 01 — The transformation

A few photographs. One remembered voice. Together, they become a place you can enter. Keepscape turns a
forgotten camera roll into a living place—without changing what really happened.

## 02 — The promise

Touch one lantern, then open Evidence Lens. The world falls back and its Truth Thread leads home: photo region,
audio timecode, human decision. Wonder gets a receipt, in plain sight.

## 03 — The source desk

GPT-5.6 finds the pattern. A person protects the truth. When the archive cannot prove the bicycle's owner,
Keepscape preserves the uncertainty.

## 04 — Codex and the host gate

GPT-5.6 maps the evidence. You approve the story. Codex sees only opaque tokens—not family prose—and builds a
bounded interaction. The public replay links to a verified live SDK receipt.

## 05 — Lantern Lane

Then the interface falls quiet. Painted. Tasseled. Pale. Three lanterns return to their sources, and the whole
archive awakens around you.

## 06 — Repair Bench

Another memory becomes another kind of play. Ring too early and the ritual resets. Turn, loosen, chain, ring—the
evidence becomes the mechanic.

## 07 — Engineering evidence

Codex also helped build Keepscape: sixty-two tests, four browser journeys, and one publicly inspectable live SDK
receipt.

## 08 — Close

Keepscape. Walk into memory—without letting AI rewrite it.
