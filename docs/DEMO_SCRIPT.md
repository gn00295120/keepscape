# 2:55 demo script

Target runtime: **2:48–2:55**. Never exceed 2:59.

## 0:00–0:13 — The loss

**Visual:** A quiet camera roll, a short waveform, then the Keepscape title.

**Narration:** “The stories that explain our family are scattered across photos, voice notes, and one person's
memory. Albums can store them. Generators can beautify them. Neither lets us enter the story without risking
that AI invents the missing pieces.”

## 0:13–0:28 — The promise

**Visual:** Hero and the two sample archives.

**Narration:** “Keepscape turns real photos and original voice into a small place your family can explore—and
every factual detail can prove where it came from.”

## 0:28–0:52 — GPT-5.6 understands the archive

**Visual:** Open the night-market archive. Move through sources, transcript timecodes, extracted claims, and
uncertainty states.

**Narration:** “GPT-5.6 connects what was said to what is visible. It emits a typed story blueprint: supported
claims, meaningful objects, emotional beats, and the gaps only a human can resolve. Model confidence is never
treated as evidence.”

## 0:52–1:09 — Human agency

**Visual:** Show the family-confirmed date beside a source-backed detail. On the separate uncertain ownership
claim, choose **Confirm as remembered** and show that the build receipt gains a human confirmation source.

**Narration:** “Before anything is generated, the storyteller must confirm or deliberately preserve every
uncertain detail. That decision becomes a new provenance source. Keepscape does not clone voices, recreate
people, or make up family history.”

## 1:09–1:30 — Codex builds and verifies

**Visual:** Build pipeline animates through interaction design, the actual API trace, hotspot allowlist, and
provenance validation; hold on Build evidence.

**Narration:** “Codex turns the approved blueprint into a bespoke typed interaction inside an isolated,
no-network workspace. The host checks the hotspot allowlist, schema, and every source reference, then returns
the actual run trace and build receipt—not arbitrary code for the browser to execute.”

## 1:30–2:07 — Emotional reveal

**Visual:** Launch Lantern Lane, 1998. Collect the first object, open its photo evidence, play the linked
timecode, complete the trail.

**Narration:** “This memory became a lantern trail. Each object reveals the original proof beneath the generated
scene. The interpretation creates a place to explore; the photos and voice remain the authority.”

## 2:07–2:31 — Prove it is not a template

**Visual:** Switch to Four Moves at the Repair Bench. Perform the repair steps out of order once, then complete them.

**Narration:** “A second archive produces a different mechanic: a repair ritual where sequence carries the
story. It is the same safe runtime, but a different typed interaction shaped by the evidence.”

## 2:31–2:49 — Codex development evidence

**Visual:** Fast montage of decision log, parallel Codex threads, tests, responsive view, and repository.

**Narration:** “GPT-5.6 powers the source-grounded blueprint. Codex is both the typed-interaction builder inside
the product and the engineering partner that implemented, tested, and visually refined Keepscape during Build
Week.”

## 2:49–2:55 — Close

**Visual:** Completed exhibit, title card.

**Narration:** “Keepscape. Your memories deserve more than a camera roll. Walk into a true story.”

## Capture checklist

- Record at 1920×1080 with browser zoom fixed at 100%.
- Show a real cursor and no jump that hides a failed state.
- Keep the OpenAI model name and Codex build receipt legible.
- Include spoken narration; captions are supplementary.
- An AI-assisted voiceover is explicitly allowed by the July 13 host announcement.
- Upload publicly to YouTube and verify playback while logged out.
